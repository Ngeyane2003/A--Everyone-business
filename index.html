Step 1: Navigate to the Developer Dashboard

What you're seeing

You're currently in PayPal's sandbox mode, which is a testing environment. This is exactly where you should be at this stage!

Note: The sandbox allows you to test payments without moving real money. You'll switch to live mode once everything is working correctly.

What to do now

Look for a menu or navigation option that says "Dashboard", "My Apps & Credentials", or "Sandbox".

Where to click:

[Screenshot would show the PayPal developer interface]

Typically, you'll find a link to your sandbox business account in the left sidebar under "Sandbox" > "Accounts".

Step 2: Create a Sandbox Business Account

Create test business account

Before getting your API credentials, you need a sandbox business account:

  1. Go to the "Sandbox" section in the left menu
  2. Click on "Accounts"
  3. Click the "Create Account" button
  4. Select "Business" as the account type
  5. Fill in the test account details (you can use fake information)
  6. Click "Create"
[Screenshot would show the account creation form]

Tip: Name your test account something recognizable like "Learn & Earn AI Test Business" so you can easily identify it later.

Step 3: Create a New App

Create your application

Now you'll create an app to get your API credentials:

  1. Go to the "Dashboard" > "My Apps & Credentials"
  2. Scroll down to the "REST API apps" section
  3. Click "Create App"
  4. Name your app (e.g., "Learn & Earn AI Website")
  5. Select the business account you created in the previous step
  6. Click "Create App"
[Screenshot would show the app creation form]

Step 4: Get Your API Credentials

Find your credentials

After creating your app, you'll see your API credentials:

  1. Find your app in the "REST API apps" list and click on it
  2. Look for the "Sandbox" tab (not Live)
  3. Find the "Client ID" and "Secret"
[Screenshot would show the Client ID and Secret]

Update your website code

Replace the placeholder Client ID in your website code:

<script src="https://www.paypal.com/sdk/js?client-id=YOUR_CLIENT_ID_HERE¤cy=USD"></script>

Important: Never expose your Secret key in frontend code. The Secret key should only be used on your server if you implement advanced PayPal features.

Step 5: Test and Go Live

Test your integration

Now it's time to test your PayPal integration:

  1. Use your sandbox business account credentials to test transactions
  2. Create a test personal account in the Sandbox > Accounts section
  3. Try making a purchase using the test personal account
  4. Verify that the transaction appears in your sandbox business account
[Screenshot would show a test transaction]

Go live

Once testing is successful, switch to live mode:

  1. Go to your app in the developer dashboard
  2. Switch from the "Sandbox" tab to the "Live" tab
  3. Get your live Client ID (it will be different from sandbox)
  4. Replace the sandbox Client ID in your website with the live one

Note: You may need to provide additional business information to PayPal to enable live transactions.

Congratulations!

You've successfully set up PayPal integration for your Learn & Earn AI website.

Your students can now securely purchase your courses using PayPal.
